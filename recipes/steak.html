Tender Pan Fried Steak

Cooking that perfect steak

Cooking a tender, juicy steak is easier than you might think. I've been cooking tender steaks with this method consistently for years. We don't need any fancy marinades or special tactics.

The list of things you need is actually quite small. Let's get started!

Ingredients

Before we list the ingredients, let's talk about what kind of steak you should use. Typically ribeye, t-bone, porterhouse or strip steak are going to be the most tender. Acquire one of those and you'll be all set!

Once you have your steak, we're going to need a good amount of butter to cook it in. We'll need a pan of course, preferrably a cast iron skillet (this compliments the steak flavor very well). I recommend only using salt on your first cooked steak to get a baseline for what you like or don't like. Salt is really all you need!

Ingredient recap;
  • Steak (ribeye, t-bone, porterhouse or strip steak)
  • Pan (preferrably cast iron skillet)
  • Butter
  • Salt (good quality pink salt works the best for me)

Steps

  1. Take your steak of choice out of the package and sprinkle your preferred amount of salt on. Let it sit while you prepare the pan.
  2. Grab your pan and put a generous amount of butter in. We want the steak to sit in the butter the whole time it's coooking.
  3. Turn the heat on medium-high and let the butter melt. This is a crucial point; let the butter get hot enough to the point that it's almost smoking. You want the pan nice and hot before you put your steak in.
